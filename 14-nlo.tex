\section{Next-to-leading order}
\label{sec:nlo}

At LO accuracy, DIS is trivial \fherror{add LO diagram} and we find
\begin{equation}
    C_q^{(0)}(z) = C_{\bar q}^{(0)}(z) = e_q^2 \bar C_q^{(0)}(z) ~\forall q\,, C_g^{(0)}(z) = 0 \label{eq:LOC}
\end{equation}
where $q$ represents any quark and $g$ represent the gluon.
Inserting \cref{eq:LOC} into \cref{eq:fact1} yields \cref{eq:LOxs}.

Starting from next-to-leading order (NLO), the diagrams become more complicated and we encounter new features.

\begin{itemize}
    \item NLOq
    \item NLOg
\end{itemize}
